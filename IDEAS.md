# Game Ideas Queue

One idea per line, starting with `-`. The factory takes the **first** idea each run, removes it, and builds it. Add your own ideas here anytime — they take priority over researched/invented ones.

Format: `- Name or concept — one line of what it is and (if multiplayer) how peers interact.`

- Turntide — draughts where a jump never kills: leap an adjacent enemy and it flips to your colour Reversi-style and stays put, so the 24 stones never leave the board and every capture swings allegiance rather than thinning the ranks; men crown into kings on the far row but a flipped king loses its crown (no king-farming), and a just-converted stone is "cooling" — immune to being re-jumped until it has sat through one enemy turn — which kills flip-flop wars, while a rout threshold (control 18 of 24 = instant win) ends swingy games decisively instead of drawing. Fully solo: play an alpha-beta AI, chase a fewest-moves rout score-attack, or a daily fixed-position "flip-the-board-in-N" puzzle whose seed also picks who moves first. Because gameplay has zero randomness, 2P peers stay in lockstep by broadcasting only the origin square and jump path each turn — both clients re-derive the identical result (flips, crown-strips, cooling flags) from the same deterministic rules via patterns/net.ts so no board state is ever sent, the host validates legality and holds authority, and patterns/rng.ts only seeds side-assignment and the daily puzzle. Competitive, so balance is the game: the runaway risk is a single long forced jump-chain flipping half the board in one turn, tuned by a per-turn conversion cap (at most K flips), forced-capture on/off, cooling duration and the rout threshold — simulate hundreds of self-play games and assert first-player win rate near 50%, a smooth P(leader-at-move-N wins) with no move-3 cliff, and a low draw/flip-loop rate before shipping.
- Lastlight — a bite-sized frozen-frontier city-builder tuned for one ~5–6 minute match: a lone Generator radiates a ring of warmth across a small hex map and you get a handful of short "days" to grow a settlement outward before an ever-dropping cold snuffs it, so WHERE things go is the whole game — Hearth-homes only shelter workers inside the warmth radius, Gathering huts must sit on the wood/fish/coal tiles they harvest, and Workshops buff only the neighbours they touch, so every drop is a placement puzzle against a closing frost. Each day you stoke the Generator (spend fuel to push the warmth ring out a tile, or hoard it and let the rim freeze), assign your few workers, and dispatch Scouts into the surrounding fog to uncover resource caches and stranded survivors who join your population — a scout out is a worker not working, so exploring is a gamble on the payoff landing before the next cold snap. Survive all N days with people still warm and fed and you score on survivors + buildings + caches banked; let the last hearth go dark and the run ends. Mobile-first, no menus: drag a building from a bottom tray onto a tile (patterns/drag.ts tap/drag classifier), tap the Generator to stoke it, tap a scout then a fog tile to send — all thumb-reachable, footer hidden while a run is live. Solo-first: a full run against a daily worldwide seed (identical map, weather curve and cache layout for everyone → async global ladder) plus freeform practice seeds. Multiplayer is a parallel same-seed race, never a shared board: 2–6 peers get the identical settlement seed from one host broadcast via patterns/rng.ts (so nobody draws a warmer map), each builds their OWN city and broadcasts only a tiny status ping (day + population + score), highest surviving score wins, and because every client sims only its own city there is nothing to desync — the host owns just seed, countdown and the final ladder (one room per session, in-room rematch via patterns/rematch.ts re-broadcasts a fresh seed, never leave+rejoin). It's you-versus-the-cold, not player-versus-player, so there's no seat advantage; the risk to gate is the DIFFICULTY CURVE, so a builder-bot sims hundreds of seeds to assert P(survive to day N) forms a smooth ramp (never trivially safe, never an unwinnable wall), that median fuel/food buffers stay tense but survivable, and that warmth-first, expand-first and explore-heavy strategies all clear at comparable rates so no single line dominates — tuning the temperature ramp, fuel burn cost, cache richness and scout payoff until skill, not the seed, decides it.
- Sporeline — an open-field maze tower-defence with a versus twist, quick matches (~6–7 min): creeps march from a gate toward your Heart across an empty grid and there IS no fixed path — the towers you drop ARE the walls, so you're mazing, snaking the route as long as you can to buy more shots, but the classic rule holds (you can never fully wall the gate off, there's always one road through), making every placement a geometry puzzle of lengthening the kill-corridor without springing a leak. Kills pay gold you split between more/upgraded towers and the signature lever — SENDING a spore into a rival's field, which drops an extra creep on them AND bumps your own income, so you're forever torn between turtling a tighter maze and pressing an economic rush. Escalating waves, last Heart still beating wins. Mobile-first, no menus: drag a tower from the bottom tray onto a cell (patterns/drag.ts tap/drag classifier), tap a tower to upgrade or sell, tap Send then a rival to attack — all thumb-reachable, footer hidden while a run is live. Solo-first: a daily-seed endless survival ladder (identical wave script for everyone → async global board on furthest wave + score) plus a skirmish versus an AI that mazes and sends back. Multiplayer is 2–4 P2P peers, desync-proof by construction: the host broadcasts one wave seed via patterns/rng.ts so every field draws the same natural waves, each peer sims ONLY its own board and broadcasts just its actions (tower place/upgrade/sell + timestamped send-events aimed at a rival) which the target client re-spawns deterministically, plus a small display-only field snapshot so you can watch a rival's maze buckle — the host owns seed, countdown and the win check (one room per session, in-room rematch via patterns/rematch.ts re-broadcasts a fresh seed, never leave+rejoin). Competitive, so balance is the gate: the danger is a snowball (early income lead → more sends → more income) stacking on any turn-order edge, so the factory sims hundreds of AI-vs-AI matches to assert P(leader-at-wave-N wins) climbs smoothly with no early lock-in, seat win rates sit 45–55%, and blowouts stay rare — tuning the send-cost curve, gold-per-kill, a trailing-player catch-up income, an interest/economy cap and the maze-length payoff until greedy-economy, aggressive-send and pure-turtle lines all stay viable.
- Rootbound — a boardless hex-tile duel with no board at all: you and an opponent draw from a hand of six creature types and place them edge-to-edge into one ever-growing shared colony, aiming to fully ring the enemy's immobile Heartroot with occupied cells while a strict "one colony" rule forbids any placement or slide that would split the cluster in two; each unit has its own crawl — the Creeper walks one cell around the rim keeping contact, the Burrower slips through any single-file gap to the far side, the Spindle jumps in a straight line over a line of touching pieces, the Warden pins an adjacent enemy so it can't move, and the Sporeling can only be placed (never moved) but converts the colour of whatever it's dropped beside — and the signature twist is polarity: a creature touching mostly your own pieces moves short and defends, but one reaching into enemy territory moves further and strikes, so you're constantly choosing to nest safe or overextend. Fully solo versus a minimax AI that reads the ring-completion threat, plus a daily async-seed puzzle with a fixed opening colony to solve. RNG-free, so P2P is exact: the host assigns seats and both peers keep identical colony state, each turn broadcast as a single {unit, from, to} action that every client re-simulates through the same legal-move and one-colony flood-fill check, with any tie-broken ordering pinned by a match-start seed via patterns/rng.ts so no board state ever crosses the wire. Competitive, so balance is gated: the risk is first-player tempo plus a possibly dominant Burrower teleporting straight to the enemy core, so the factory sims hundreds of AI-vs-AI games to assert first-player win rate stays near even (levers: the opener must place their Heartroot before drawing a second unit, and a Burrower gap-slip costs that turn's placement) and that ring-surround endings don't collapse into unbreakable stalemate draws.
