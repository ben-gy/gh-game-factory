- Boxbox — a pit-stop TRIAGE game where the decision is the game and the tapping is only the execution: a car is inbound every few seconds and before it arrives you get partial telemetry (tyre temps and wear, fuel, brake temps, wing damage) plus a garbled driver radio line that is subjective and sometimes plain wrong ("front's gone", "I can't turn left"), and you choose which jobs to actually do — every job you add costs seconds NOW and every job you skip costs seconds across the stint, so a full service is never the right answer and neither is the minimum. The stop itself is a dependency-ordered tap sequence (jack up before wheels off, wheels off before wheels on, gun clear before jack down) where the wrong order wastes time or causes an unsafe release. Co-op is the strong shape: each player owns crew roles (front jack / left side / right side / fuel and wing) with dependencies that cross players, so nobody can solo it — one pair of hands cannot hold the jack and gun a wheel — and the whole crew shares one clock. Versus runs the identical stop side by side under the same clock, fastest VALID stop wins, and an unsafe release is a time penalty rather than an instant loss so a bad stop is recoverable. Balance gate: the triage layer must demonstrably beat raw tapping speed — sim a bot that reads the telemetry and triages against one that always does the full service and assert the triaging bot wins by a real margin, because if it does not this is a twitch test with a story painted on it. Second gate: the radio line must be worth listening to, so measure that a bot ignoring radio does measurably worse than one that weighs it against the telemetry, or the whole fiction is decorative.

- Undercut — race strategy with no driving at all: you pick a starting compound and call pit windows while the race simulates forward at watchable speed, where tyres have a wear curve and a temperature window, fuel load costs lap time, and weather arrives on a FORECAST that is a probability rather than a fact. The undercut versus the overcut is the central decision — pit early into clean air and hope to leapfrog, or stay out on dying rubber and hope the rain comes — and because you cannot drive, every place you gain or lose is attributable to a call you made. A season strings races into a tournament with points and a LIMITED compound allocation carried between weekends, so a greedy race mortgages the next one. Multiplayer: async-seed is the natural fit (identical race, identical weather seed, compare finishing position and the two strategies side by side), plus live P2P for 2-6 peers running the same deterministic race simultaneously and watching each other's pit calls land in real time — peers exchange only pit decisions, a few bytes each, so it is tiny and structurally desync-proof. Balance gate is the DOMINANT STRATEGY trap: if one compound plan wins regardless of weather there is no game, so the sim must prove at least three genuinely distinct strategies each take a meaningful share of wins across weather draws with no single plan above roughly 40%, and must check that the forecast's uncertainty actually changes the optimal call rather than being flavour text over a solved race. The results screen should show the retrospectively optimal strategy on your exact seed, so you always learn what you left on the table.
