=== Web Games ===

Unstrung — A word game about making the board rather than reading it. You get one long strand of letters; find a run of touching letters that spells a word and cut it out, and fresh thread is spliced straight into the gap. The two joins it leaves are seams, they are permanent and visible, and a word that spans one scores TRIPLE. So every cut is a choice about what the strand can spell next — and the seam you leave behind is the one your opponent gets to use, which makes taking the small word instead of the big one a real decision from the first move. The free play is always there (the fresh thread is a short word) but it is the cheap one; the points are in the bridges you build. Everyone gets the same number of cuts, and the results screen shows the best line a solver could find on your exact strand. Uses a curated list of everyday words, not a tournament dictionary, so obscure three-letter words do not count. Three modes: Strand, Deepcut (nothing under four letters), and Twinfold (two strands at once). Solo, against the machine, a daily strand, or peer-to-peer for two to four. [word, versus] https://unstrung.benrichardson.dev

Undercut — Race strategy with no driving at all. The race runs itself forward and your only move is the pit call: box now, and onto which tyre. Every place you gain or lose comes down to a decision — the undercut (pit early onto fresh rubber and leapfrog a rival stuck on old tyres) versus the overcut (stay out on a dying set and hope the rain comes). Read the track temperature, which decides the fastest compound, and in the wet-capable races a rain forecast that is a probability, not a fact, firming as the window nears — so a 40% forecast really does rain about 40% of the time, and betting early is a genuine gamble. Push a set past its cliff and it falls off a shelf; the wrong tyre for the conditions is a disaster. Three modes: a dry Sprint, a coin-flip Grand Prix, and a volatile Deluge. Solo against the field, an identical-seed share, or peer-to-peer for up to six ranked by time. [strategy, versus] https://undercut.benrichardson.dev

Boxbox —A pit-stop game where the call is everything and the tapping is only how you carry it out. The car comes in with partial telemetry — tyres, fuel, front wing, brakes — and a driver on the radio who is subjective and sometimes plain wrong, and you choose which jobs the crew does before it arrives. Every job you add costs seconds in the stop; every fault you leave costs seconds on track; and a system you send out in the red blows up. A full service is never right and neither is the bare minimum — you triage. Then you run the stop itself, jacking up before the wheels come off and hitting GO only when it's all clear. Lowest total time wins, and the results show the perfect line on your exact seed. Solo against a field, or peer-to-peer for up to six on the identical race. [strategy, versus] https://boxbox.benrichardson.dev

Rootbound — There is no board, only pieces, and they all touch. Every piece from both players forms one single colony that you may never split in two, and you win by filling all six cells around your rival's Heartroot — with pieces of either colour, so walling your own Heartroot in is how you lose. The twist is polarity: a piece surrounded by your own colour is safe and shuffles one cell, while a piece outnumbered by the enemy is exposed and moves its full range. Range is not a property of a piece, it is a property of where it stands, so retreating is slow and overextending is fast. Solo against a three-strength engine, or peer-to-peer with a friend. [board, versus] https://rootbound.benrichardson.dev

Sporeline — Open-field maze defence with no fixed path: the towers you drop ARE the walls, so every placement is about folding the creeps' road back on itself to buy your guns more seconds. You can never fully seal the Gate — that placement is simply rejected — so you are always shaping a corridor rather than closing one, and the game previews the road you would create before you commit. Kills pay gold you split between a tighter maze and sending a spore into a rival's field, which drops a creep on them and raises your income for the rest of the match. Last Heart beating wins. Solo against bots, or peer-to-peer for up to four. [strategy, versus] https://sporeline.benrichardson.dev

Lastlight — One generator is all that stands between your settlement and the freeze, and only what sits inside its ring of warmth actually works. Homes shelter nobody outside it, gathering huts must sit on the very tile they harvest, and workshops boost only the neighbours they touch — so every placement is a puzzle against a closing frost. Burn coal to push the ring back out, but a bigger ring costs more to hold. Solo against the cold, or race friends on the identical map. [strategy, versus] https://lastlight.benrichardson.dev

Turntide — Draughts with one rule replaced: a jump never kills. Leap an adjacent enemy and it flips to your colour and stays exactly where it is, so all 24 stones remain on the board and every capture swings two at once. Material circulates instead of accumulating, which means an early lead is not a won game. Kings can't be farmed (a flipped king loses its crown) and controlling 18 of the 24 stones wins instantly. Solo against a three-strength engine, or peer-to-peer with a friend. [board, versus] https://turntide.benrichardson.dev

Changeling — Real chess, all the normal rules, win by checkmate — with one rule bolted on: the instant your piece captures, it turns into whatever it just captured. Take a pawn with your queen and your queen becomes a pawn; ram a pawn into their queen and that pawn is a queen. Only the king is immune. Solo against an engine, or peer-to-peer with a friend. [board, versus] https://changeling.benrichardson.dev

Frostward — A creeping frost spreads only into the wind's forward arc, so it grows as a cone that sweeps the board like a searchlight. Everyone secretly plays one card a turn and every Veer adds up, which means the only way to get the cone off your own hearths is to point it at somebody else's. Last hearth still lit wins. [board, versus] https://frostward.benrichardson.dev

Deepwatch — A co-op game of nerve played in total silence: surface a hand of deep-sea creatures onto one line, shallowest first, with no numbers anywhere and no chat. A card's height on the depth gauge is its depth, and the only thing you know about anyone else is how long they've been holding. [card, co-op] https://deepwatch.benrichardson.dev
