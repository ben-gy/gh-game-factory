## Idea Source
**Invented.** `IDEAS.md` was empty, so the gap in `registry.json` picked the genre: arcade, puzzle,
board, card, word and party were all represented — **strategy** had nothing.

The brief was one fresh, legible mechanic. Windup's is *simultaneous programming*: commit three
movement cards blind, then everyone's program executes at once, one step at a time, and bots shove
each other. The reveal is the product.

The second mechanic — **ripening cogs** (+1 value per round, capped at 5) — was chosen deliberately
against principle #18's hard-won lesson ("make the early game small and the late game big"). It is a
pure function of `round - bornRound`: zero new state, so P2P sync is untouched, and it makes the
late game exciting rather than capping the leader. It worked on the first measurement.

Rejected as too close to existing games: territory capture (hexbloom), simultaneous secret bidding
(bidstorm), deduction (nightwire), beam routing (lumenlock). A *race* was rejected on principle —
in a race, being ahead intrinsically compounds, which is the snowball #18 forbids. Scoring by
collection under a ripening clock keeps "leader" fluid.

## Game Details
- **Name / repo:** Windup / ben-gy/windup
- **Genre:** strategy
- **Core loop:** Slot 3 cards → everyone executes simultaneously → shove, grab cogs, fall in pits →
  cogs left behind ripen → most value when the rounds run out wins.
- **Multiplayer:** live P2P, 2–4 players. Host-authoritative **commit/resolve** — because
  `resolveRound` is pure, the host broadcasts *programs* (`res`), never truth; every peer computes
  the identical next state. Channels: `pg` (program), `res` (round result), `snap` (re-anchor).
  Plus solo vs bots and an async seed-share link.
- **Stack / render:** vanilla-ts / canvas arena + DOM HUD, cards, menus
- **Engine modules used:** net, rematch, lobby, rng, sound, storage, identity, mobile (+ mobile.css).
  Not loop.ts — the sim is turn-resolved, not fixed-timestep.

## Balance — the sim refereed, and the diagnosis would have been wrong
Built `tests/balance.test.ts` **before any tuning**, per principle #18. Baseline (bot-vs-bot,
fixed seeds):

- **Leader curve is healthy on the first try.** 4P Clockwork: P(leader wins) 30% at r1, 34% at r3,
  36% at r4 (chance = 25%), rising to 62% at r8 and 100% at r12. 2P: 51 / 57 / 58 early → 82% at
  r10. Flat and near chance early, decisive only at the end — the shape the principle asks for.
- **Sweeps said "change nothing", which is the point of measuring.** ripeCap 3/5/7/9: a bigger cap
  only bought blowouts (25% → 31%) with no curve improvement. Cog density: carpeting the board cut
  blowouts to 18% but left 128 points of value lying around — a buffet, not a contest. centreEvery
  2/3/4: the shipped 3 was already best (24% vs 26%/27%). Every "improvement" I might have argued
  for was worse.
- **The one real bug the sim found: a 3-player seat bias.** Seat 2 won **38.1%** against an expected
  33.3% over 1200 matches. Structural, not tunable: a square grid's rotation-only symmetry has no
  map from corner 0 to corner 2, so 3-of-4 corners can never be symmetric. Fixed by dealing the
  corners from the shared seed (a dealer button). After: 36.3 / 33.9 / 36.0.
- **Feel measured, not just the curve** (#18: a fix that flattens the joy is a failed run): 46% of
  collected cogs are worth ≥4 and 33% are taken at the full cap of 5. Cogs genuinely ripen and get
  fought over — the core verb survives.
- Turn-0 fairness is structural: 90° rotationally symmetric board, starts on one rotation orbit,
  cogs spawn in symmetric orbits + a contested centre cog on the fixed point.
- Gauntlet's 4 slots mean a 6-choose-4 = 360-program bot search: **measured at ~8ms/match** vs
  Clockwork's ~1ms *before* committing to the bigger mode, so it runs 150 matches, not 600.

## Juice
Tweened simultaneous execution at 460ms/step (the reveal is the product), eased motion, brass spark
bursts on a shove, value-scaled cog bursts, dust plume + big shake on a pit fall, hit-stop on
impact, score pops, a pulsing glow on cogs ripened to 4+, a rotating centre cog, procedural SFX per
action, `sfx.unlock()` on first gesture. All degrade under `prefers-reduced-motion` (no shake, no
hit-stop, thinner particles — but never silent, because a cog burst is feedback, not decoration).

## Mutation testing (every fix seen RED before green)
1. **3P seat fix** — reverted `seatSlots`' seeded shuffle. The obvious outcome test (*"every seat
   wins ~1/3"*) **stayed GREEN** — the ~2.4-point bias is unmeasurable against a 600-match sample's
   ~1.9-point standard error. That test was a guess. Rewrote it to assert the **mechanism** (each
   seat must draw each corner ~25% of the time); the mutation then failed at `expected 0.75 to be
   less than 0.03`. Kept the win-rate test as a loose backstop for a hexbloom-scale disaster.
2. **Host takeover** — made `setHost` a no-op (the rhythm-relay shape). RED: survivor never
   finishes.
3. **Solo visibility pause** — disabled it. RED: `expected 9 to be 1` — nine rounds burned.

## Bugs found by actually playing it (none were visible to the tests)
1. **A staged program was thrown away on timeout.** Filled all three slots, the clock expired before
   I hit Lock in, and a bot played the round instead. The timeout exists to stop an *absent* player
   holding the room, not to punish a present one for being slow on the last tap. Fixed: auto-send
   the staged program 1.8s before the deadline.
2. **The tray went stale during the replay** — a locked program shown next to the *next* round's
   hand. Now the tray shows the program that actually ran, step by step, read back off the resolved
   timeline (honest even when the clock played you).
3. **The board resized between planning and replay** (the tray loses its hand row). Pinned a
   min-height; jump measured 0.
4. **Solo game burned rounds in a backgrounded tab.** The round clock is a `setInterval` and keeps
   running while rAF — and so the replay — is paused. Fixed with a solo-only `pauseWhen`.
   Deliberately NOT wired in multiplayer, where a unilateral pause would strand the room.
5. **Game flow was gated on rAF.** The replay animates on rAF (correct — it's a visual), but the
   next planning phase only opened when the reveal ended, so a backgrounded tab left the round
   half-revealed forever. Fixed with a `setInterval` wall-clock guard: rAF for pixels, timers for
   time. This is the factory's own principle, and it also made the game headlessly verifiable —
   without it the local playthrough could not complete.
