## Idea Source
IDEAS.md, first entry (removed on pickup):

> Blob arena (agar.io-like) — you're a circle on a shared petri-dish map eating
> scattered pellets to grow; any blob can swallow a smaller one whole on overlap,
> bigger blobs move slower, and dying respawns you small so nobody is ever out. …
> Distinct from snake-royale: mass-based swallowing and split/eject mechanics.

Fleshed into **Morsel**: the same eat-or-be-eaten frame, but with the classic
agar failure mode designed out. The one added verb is **dash** — a burst that
costs 15% of your mass, sprayed behind you as food for whoever you're chasing —
so the only way to grow a lead is to spend it. No split/eject (would blur the one
verb and double the netcode surface).

## Game Details
- **Name / repo:** Morsel / ben-gy/morsel
- **Genre:** arcade (free-for-all io-style)
- **Core loop:** eat pellets → swallow smaller blobs → run from bigger ones;
  big = slow, so catching anyone costs you a dash (−15% mass, sprayed as food);
  pellets bloom in value over the round so the last stretch decides it; biggest
  at the whistle wins; being eaten costs 1.5s, nobody is out.
- **Multiplayer:** live P2P free-for-all, 2–8, **split authority** (each peer
  owns its own blob's motion + death; host owns pellets, bots, clock, ledger);
  plus solo-vs-bots and async seed-share.
- **Stack / render:** vanilla-ts / Canvas 2D
- **Engine modules used:** net, rematch, lobby, rng, loop, input, sound (adapted),
  storage, mobile, identity, countdown

## The balance work (principle #18 — build the sim FIRST)
The design document confidently predicted a **snowball** (leader gets biggest,
can't be caught, wins). The AI-vs-AI sim, built and baselined before tuning,
refuted it: Morsel's disease was the **opposite** — `P(leader at t=90% wins)` was
only **30%** in Petri, the lead changed **115 times** in 150s, and there were
just **7.5 swallows** a round across 6 blobs. Chasing was strictly irrational
(the dash, ramped through ACCEL, closed less ground than the cooldown gave back),
so nobody ever ate anyone and the winner was whoever touched the last pellet.

Measured fixes (all pinned in `tests/balance.test.ts` / `src/tuning.ts`):
- **Dash as an impulse** (set velocity directly) at 3.2× for 0.6s, not a ramped
  target speed → swallows ~7.5 → ~13, P(leader@90%) 30% → ~65%.
- **SPEED_EXP = 0.24** — how much size costs you. Swept 0.12→0.30; a smooth
  trade-off (0.12 → 42% blowouts and the snowball is real down there; 0.30 →
  drains the swallows out). Chosen for the flattest early curve with the game
  still eating itself.
- **Seat fairness bug found by the sim** (like Hexbloom): iterating blobs in
  index order handed seat 0 every contested pellet — ~6 pts of win rate, χ² p≈0.02.
  Fixed with a per-step rotation (`serveOrder`). The seat SWEEP did NOT catch it
  (reverting left it green), so the mechanism has its own mutation-tested unit
  test in `game.test.ts`.
- **Skirmish** was a farming sim (5 swallows, halftime leader won 43% vs 25%
  chance). Root cause measured: 4 blobs in a small dish isn't crowded. Six blobs
  → 14 swallows, the liveliest mode.

Final shape (300–800 rounds/mode): P(leader wins) near chance early, ~60–71% at
t=90%; every seat within a few points of `100/seats`; blowouts ≤28%.

## Bug found & fixed during the live smoke test
The two-tab test surfaced a real **rematch soft-deadlock on the rejoin path**:
after a host transfer, the peer who left and rejoined got a fresh round counter
(0) while the incumbent was at round N, so their votes were dropped as stale and
neither could ready up. Fixed in `rematch.ts` — a peer now adopts the **host's**
round number (authoritative for the counter) via a `cur` field on votes.
Mutation-tested regression added ("lets a peer that LEFT and rejoined mid-match
ready up again"); reverting the fix goes red. NB: this lives in the shared
`patterns/rematch.ts` too — worth porting back.
