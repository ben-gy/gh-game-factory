## Idea Source
IDEAS.md (first in queue): *"Boxbox — a pit-stop TRIAGE game where the decision
is the game and the tapping is only the execution… every job you add costs
seconds NOW and every job you skip costs seconds across the stint… Co-op is the
strong shape… Balance gate: the triage layer must demonstrably beat raw tapping
speed… the radio line must be worth listening to."*

## Game Details
- **Name / repo:** Boxbox / ben-gy/boxbox
- **Genre:** strategy
- **Core loop:** triage the car's four systems from noisy telemetry + a subjective
  radio → run a dependency-ordered stop → watch the stint cost the calls → repeat;
  lowest total time wins.
- **Multiplayer:** live P2P versus (2–6) on one shared seed + async seed-share +
  solo-vs-AI-field. Parallel same-seed race: peers exchange only a status ping +
  a final result, so there is no shared state to desync.
- **Stack / render:** vanilla-ts / DOM
- **Engine modules used:** net, rematch, turn, lobby (+ qr), rng, sound, storage,
  mobile, feedback (engine v1.2.1)

## Juice
Procedural SFX on every action (gun/jack/fuel/radio/alarm/GO/win/lose), the stop
clock counting up in a mono readout, station lights blocked→ready-pulse→done,
condition bars filling on arrival, screen shake on an unsafe release and on a
catastrophe reveal, green/amber/red condition ladder (colour + label + fill

## The design decisions the balance sim refereed (principle #18)
- **GATE 1 — triage beats tapping (PROVED):** a telemetry-triaging bot beats a
  do-everything bot by ~15s (Sprint), ~24s (Enduro), ~13s (Monsoon), winning >70%
  of paired races. Doing nothing (`min`) is far worse (catastrophes). Mutation-
  verified.
- **GATE 2 — the radio matters (PROVED, after one wrong turn):** the FIRST radio
  bot obeyed the driver even against a live gauge and was NET NEGATIVE in
  Sprint/Enduro (over-fixing on false alarms, radio ~1-in-4 wrong). The fix: scale
  the radio's weight by telemetry UNCERTAINTY — tiny when the gauge is live, large
  when the sensor is dark. Then radio halves catastrophes and saves ~1.5s in
  Monsoon (sensors dark 24%) and is neutral where telemetry is reliable — exactly
  the design story. Forcing the bot to ignore the radio turns the Monsoon gate red
  (mutation-verified).
- **The mechanism audit was TAUTOLOGICAL at first (principle #21):** it imported
  the game's own `penaltyFor`/`isCatastrophe` and compared the game's arithmetic
  against itself, so mutating the penalty formula left it GREEN. Rewrote the audit
  to re-derive every cost rule from its OWN independent constants; now mutating the
  penalty by +0.01 turns 3 audits red. This is the exact trap #21 warns about,
  caught by mutation-testing the guard rather than trusting it.
- The cost model **decomposes per system** (stop time additive per job; penalties
  per system; escalation couples a system only to itself), so the retrospectively-
  optimal line is an exact per-system search — both the balance baseline and the
  "here's the perfect line on your seed" on the results screen.

  rewrote to re-derive from independent constants (mutation-verified).
