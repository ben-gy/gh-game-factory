## The new core: a deterministic tyre/fuel/weather sim (`src/sim.ts`)
Lap time = BASE + fuel(fuelFrac) + grip(compound, age, wetness) + trackEvo. Grip
folds a compound's fresh pace, degradation (linear then a quadratic CLIFF past a
per-set life), and a condition penalty (slicks catastrophic wet, inters best damp,
wets best flooded). A **track-temperature** window (drawn per race) shifts which
compound is fastest — softs love a cool track, hards need heat — so the optimal
plan varies by seed. Weather is a **calibrated Bernoulli forecast**: a probability
p is drawn and shown, rain is sampled ~Bernoulli(p), and the displayed p firms
toward the truth as the window nears. Everything (weather, temp, per-set jitter) is
seed-drawn up front, so two peers on the same seed face a byte-identical race.

## Balance — the sim was built FIRST and refereed (principle #18)
The initial model was a solved race and the sim proved it, overruling the design:
- **Sprint was 100% one plan** ('start medium, one-stop late onto softs'). Fix was
  structural, not a dial: **track temperature**, which makes the optimal start +
  strategy vary by seed (cold→soft, hot→hard).
- **The forecast was pure flavour** — reacting to real rain always beat pre-empting
  it, so a "gamble on rain" plan never won. Fix: a **one-lap reaction lag** (a
  reactor drives a lap in real water before it can box, using last lap's felt
  wetness), which makes a confident forecast worth pre-empting; plus a faster rain
  onset.
- **A same-compound pit was silently dropped** by a `call !== compound` guard, so
  the entire soft→soft cold-track plan scored 0% and ate the mandatory-stop
  penalty. A pit is a pit even onto the same compound; fixed.
Final at n=500/mode: no fixed strategy > 45% and ≥3 take ≥10% (Sprint top ~38%, GP
~38%, Deluge ~30%); the best plan when it RAINS ≠ when it stays DRY (proving the
forecast changes the optimal call); and the hindsight-optimal that can pre-empt
onto wets beats the best dry-only line by ~40s/race on rain seeds.
