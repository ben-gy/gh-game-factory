# Build Log: Unstrung
**Date:** 2026-07-22
**Status:** deployed

## Idea Source
**Invented.** `IDEAS.md` was empty. The catalogue was thin on **word** games (one, `cipher-clash`, a
Boggle grid), so the brief was a word game with a genuinely new, one-sentence mechanic.

The mechanic: a strand of letters you cut contiguous words out of, where the *cut itself* changes what
the board can spell. The seam — the join left behind — scoring triple is what makes a cut an act of
manufacturing rather than harvesting, and (this turned out to be the entire balance story) what makes
the game fair, because a seam is something the *previous* player made.

## Game Details
- **Name / repo:** Unstrung / ben-gy/unstrung
- **Genre:** word
- **Core loop:** cut a contiguous word out of the strand → fresh thread splices into the gap → the two
  joins are permanent seams → a word spanning a seam scores triple → repeat until your cuts run out.
- **Multiplayer:** live P2P 2–4, **lockstep** (no board state on the wire) + async seed-share + UTC
  daily strand. Host owns only the turn clock.
- **Stack / render:** vanilla-ts / DOM
- **Engine modules:** net, rematch, turn, lobby (+qr), rng, sound, storage, mobile, identity, feedback

## Juice
Fresh tiles animate in with an overshoot; score pops rise from the cut with a spark burst (amber and
denser for a seam bridge); screen shake on any cut worth ≥40; a distinct `powerup` chime for a seam
bridge vs `coin` for a plain cut; the word chip previews the score and the `seam ×3` badge live as you
drag; 3-2-1-GO with audio before a shared round. All animations are CSS keyframes that end on their
own and every FX node is removed by `setTimeout` — never by an animation callback, which a hidden tab
never fires.

## Errors & Resolutions

### The balance sim overruled the design five times
Built first, before any tuning, exactly as principle #18 requires — and it was worth it, because the
design as planned was not a game.

1. **A strand you simply consume lasted 3.7 cuts.** A greedy player spends ~6 letters a bite, so 26
   letters is three moves. Seat 0 won 90–95% and the signature seam rule fired on 6–12% of cuts.
2. **Capping the cut length was not enough** (90% → 64% of decided games). The real killer was that the
   ply count is frequently **odd**, so seat 0 takes one more cut than seat 1 — a missing rule, not a
   bias. Fixed with a fixed number of cuts *each*, which required the strand to stop being consumable.
3. **Refilling from the tail rotted the board** — 21% of 2P and up to 100% of 4P rounds ended dry,
   because cuts happen where words are and the head became unreadable stubs. Splicing fresh thread in
   **at the cut** took the seam share 17% → 46%.
4. **Refilling with a word as long as the cut measured perfectly and was a dead game**: 0% dry, 0%
   blowouts, seats 38.0/39.5 — and 23% draws, 100% at seamMult 1. Only the draw rate could see it.
   The refill is now a guaranteed *short* word plus ribbon, so the free move is worth 9.
5. **Snake draft and start-seat rotation both made 4P worse**, refuting the obvious fix and proving the
   residual bias is not made of pick order.

The load-bearing lever is `SEAM_MULT`, swept and pinned: ×1 → seat 0 wins 77%, ×2 → 63%, ×3 → 52%.

### The refill word was an empty string for two hours
`refillWord(..., minLen=3)` returned `''` because the source list was generated from length 4 up. The
board dried out and every *outcome* metric read it as "short rounds" — indistinguishable from a hard
game. Caught by asserting the mechanism (dry rate at zero tolerance) rather than the outcome, and now
pinned by a dictionary-shape assertion that every mode's cuttable lengths have a source pool.

### A signed-shift bug made the refill `undefined`
`h ^ (h >>> 13)` yields a *signed* 32-bit int, so the pool index went negative. Fixed with `>>> 0` and
pinned by a 400-seed loop.

### Solo had no scoreboard at all
`sessionSeats` was only populated on the multiplayer path, so the solo HUD rendered empty and the
player could not see their own score. jsdom renders an empty HUD without complaint, so no unit test
saw it; the first real 375px screenshot did. Fixed, plus a structural backstop that derives placeholder
seats rather than ever painting nothing.

### `pointerdown` hit-tested with `elementFromPoint`, which returns null in a background tab
The worst bug of the run and the one only the two-tab smoke test could find. The guest's taps were
silently dropped **with the board visible, the HUD saying "Your turn", the chip saying "Drag across
letters" and a clean console** — indistinguishable from a frozen game. `pointerdown` now reads
`event.target`, which *is* the tile; `elementFromPoint` is used only during a drag, where pointer
capture makes it unavoidable, and is guarded for absence. Pinned by `tests/input.test.ts`, which runs
in jsdom where `elementFromPoint` does not exist at all — the broken environment reproduced exactly.
**Mutation-verified: reverting to hit-testing turns 4 tests red.**

### The player badge painted its numeral on itself
`background: currentColor` plus a `color` for the glyph cannot be both things at once — the HUD showed
a dark disc and the results screen an amber-on-amber invisible numeral. Fill is now set inline and
pinned in `ui.test.ts`, which matters because `contrast.test.ts` deliberately accepts four
hue-separated player colours whose luminances are only 1.06:1 apart — that is only safe *because* the
numeral carries the meaning.

### Not live-verified
The survivor playing all the way to the results screen after the host left, and the rematch vote. Each
auto-play waits out a 45s turn clock, so a full round after a host departure takes ~9 minutes of real
time. Promotion (epoch 2), continued live play, and the promoted host's clock driving the departed
seat forward were all confirmed live; reaching game-over and the rematch protocol are covered by
`takeover.test.ts` (mutation-verified — making `setHost` a no-op turns 3 red), `rematch.test.ts`,
`no-deadlock.test.ts`, `host-election.test.ts` and `net-lifecycle.test.ts`.
