## Idea Source
IDEAS.md (first item, now removed):
> Loudness escalation — one phone passed around a group in public: each player in
> turn must say the word slightly LOUDER than the last, the phone measures it with an
> AnalyserNode, and the first to bottle it (or to fail to beat the rising threshold)
> loses. Nothing is recorded, nothing is understood, nothing is uploaded — it is one
> number against a moving bar. Needs a keyboard/touch fallback (a nerve-holding bar
> you have to release at the right moment) for anyone without a mic or who denies
> permission. See principle #23.

## The input question (principle #23)
Answered **microphone loudness**, not touch. The prior 28 registry entries are every
one of them finger-on-glass or keyboard — a strong signal to reach for a sensor. The
input here is the player's actual voice volume (`AnalyserNode` RMS → 0–100 level),
compared against a rising bar. Touch was considered and rejected: a tap can't make
you self-conscious in a room full of people, which *is* the game. A full tap/keyboard
fallback exists and is a real game (build it first).

## Game Details
- **Name / repo:** Uproar / ben-gy/uproar
- **Genre:** party
- **Core loop:** pass-the-phone knockout — beat a rising loudness bar on your turn or
  you're out; last one standing wins. Solo = a personal ladder.
- **Multiplayer:** **none** (local hot-seat 2–8 + solo). No network by design —
  documented honestly in the plan/PR: the appeal is a group in one room, and
  principle #23 forbids audio on the wire, so a remote opponent would be a number on
  a screen, strictly worse. Not a gap; the right shape.
- **Stack / render:** vanilla-ts / DOM (canvas only for the confetti burst)
- **Engine modules used:** sound, storage, mobile (+ mobile.css), feedback. No
  net/rematch/lobby/rng (single-device).

## Architecture that makes a sensor game testable
- `loudness.ts` — pure derivation: `rms`, `levelFromRms` (dB), `calibrateFloorDb`,
  `peak`/`holdMs`/`steady`, `evaluateAttempt`. Unit-tested hard.
- `mic.ts` (MicSource) and `tap.ts` (TapSource) both expose `level()` and feed the
  SAME evaluator, so the game never knows which input was used → no-mic is a real
  game, never a wall.
- `attempt.ts` — countdown → measurement window → evaluate. A synthetic-feed hook
  (`window.__uproarFeed`) replays a level series so the pipeline is provable in a
  browser with no mic.

## Three modes (real spread, principle #14)
- **Bellow** — peak loudness ≥ a rising bar. Burst of nerve.
- **Sustain** — hold above a line for a *lengthening* duration. Breath control.
- **Pinpoint** — land the settled level inside a rising, *narrowing* band. Control.

## Juice
Live glowing meter with target line/band, confetti burst on a clear + party win,
screen shake + buzzer on a fail, 3-2-1-GO countdown with audio, `navigator.vibrate`
haptics (a second sensor). Respects `prefers-reduced-motion` and the mute toggle.

## Errors & Resolutions
- **rAF hang (found in the in-browser pass, fixed):** the live measurement window ran
  on `requestAnimationFrame`, which is paused in a throttled/backgrounded tab. rAF
  never fired in the verification browser and the attempt hung forever — the exact
  class of failure the factory's realtime lessons warn about. Rewrote the window onto
  `setInterval` (sampling + guaranteed end), so a phone that locks mid-shout can't
  strand the round. Pinned by a test that spies rAF and asserts it is never called;
  reverting to rAF hangs the fake-timer test.
- **Sustain/Pinpoint too hard at the start (found by the balance sim):** 2-rung games
  with a large late-seat bias. Widened the ramps (start trivially easy, cross a
  typical ability only after ~13 rungs). Every seat then within 8 pts of chance.

## Not verified (stated plainly)
A real-device microphone check is **not possible in this pipeline** — browser
automation cannot shout. The mic derivation is covered by unit tests and the
synthetic-feed browser hook; the tap fallback (the path most desktop players take, and
this environment has no mic) was played in full, on localhost and on production.
