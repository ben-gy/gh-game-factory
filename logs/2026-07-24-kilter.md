## Idea Source
IDEAS.md, the first queued line — physics tower stacking "in the shape of Tricky Towers":
> The signature rule: the bricks are RIGID BODIES, not grid cells. Nothing snaps to a grid,
> nothing ever locks, and no row ever clears — you drop a tetromino-ish brick, it falls with
> real gravity and friction, and it settles wherever momentum and the pile beneath it leave it:
> leaning, teetering, sliding off. … you lose by TOPPLING, not by filling a space.

Taken as a mechanic, not an identity — original name (Kilter), original procedural art, and the
shipped game never references the source. The line explicitly asked the run to say why this is new
against `ballast` rather than log it as an expansion: ballast is a cellular grid where rows clear,
the stack is rigid and never moves, and its "tilt" is a rendering + reachability rule; Kilter has no
grid, no clears, the stack itself IS the physics sim, and the failure condition is a real collapse.

**Input question (principle #23):** answered "touch, on purpose." Positioning a brick on a continuous
axis is a precise pointing task; the finger is the right controller. The previous build (uproar) was
the sensor game, so the ~1-in-3 sensor cadence was freshly satisfied — touch here is a deliberate
choice, not a default.

## Game Details
- **Name / repo:** Kilter / ben-gy/kilter
- **Genre:** arcade
- **Core loop:** slide to aim, tap to spin, drop; a rigid brick falls and settles wherever physics
  leaves it; build tall before the tower topples.
- **Multiplayer:** live P2P versus 2–6 as a parallel same-seed race (no tower state on the wire) +
  solo + daily seed + async share-a-tower.
- **Stack / render:** vanilla-ts / canvas 2D + matter.js physics.
- **Engine modules used:** net, rematch, turn, lobby (+ qr), rng, sound, storage, mobile, identity,
  feedback. NOT loop/input/joystick/drag (custom fixed-step physics + bespoke drag-to-aim).

## Physics engine decision (recorded in plan.md)
matter.js over a hand-rolled sequential-impulse solver. Stable stacking of compound (non-convex
tetromino) bodies is exactly matter.js's strong suit; a one-pass custom solver risked jitter that
would turn the game into a slot machine (principle #18a). A **determinism spike was run BEFORE
building on it**: identical initial conditions under a fixed 60Hz timestep reproduced to 6 decimals
(r1===r2), and a tiny perturbation diverged (skill-sensitive but reproducible). Cross-peer
determinism is deliberately NOT relied on — each peer sims only its own tower, so float divergence
between machines is harmless.

## Juice
Dust-puff particles at each contact (scaled by impact speed), screen shake by impact speed, a
near-topple slow-mo beat + red vignette, a camera that eases up the tower as it climbs, wind streaks
on an incoming gust, and procedural audio (blip/hit/coin/powerup/lose) with a mute toggle.

## Errors & Resolutions
- Physics attrition too high on first tuning (steady bot lost 38/40 bricks in Ascent): reduced the
  spawn gap (3.4→1.7 cells), lowered gravity (1.55→1.2) and raised friction so gentle placements
  stick; then the sim showed goal 9 was unreachable, so cut it to 7. Both driven by the sim, not vibes.
- TypeScript persisted a stale narrowing of `this.phase` across the many methods that mutate it
  (settle/lose/finish) → added a `phaseNow()` call-boundary helper.
- `PLAY_W` lived in game.ts, not palette — fixed the render import.
- TLS cert propagation delay on deploy (unresolved at hand-off) — see Deployment.
